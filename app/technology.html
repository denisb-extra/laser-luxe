Is an acronym for “Light Amplification by the Stimulated Emission of Radiation” which produces a sharp, focused light beam that melts a very small area of metal. The benefit of this technology is that very little heat is generated at the weld point, allowing users to easily weld microns away from the most complicated and intricate component parts without damaging heat sensitive materials.

Micro laser welding is used for joining high value, miniature components in a range of industries such as electronics, telecom, automotive, the medical industry and more. Typical applications include catheters or implants, orthodontic brackets, fiber optic couplings to laser diodes, hermetic seals, watch part components and other photonic fabrication devices.
